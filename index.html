Radio Controlled Car

A radio-controlled car, often abbreviated as RC car, is a small model vehicle that can be controlled remotely using a handheld transmitter or a mobile app. These cars are powered by batteries or fuel engines and are popular for recreational and competitive purposes. They come in various scales, from miniature to large, and can be used for racing, off-roading, or simply as a hobby for enthusiasts of all ages. RC cars typically use radio signals to transmit commands to the vehicle, allowing operators to steer, accelerate, and brake from a distance.
